The Tritone

Dave Anderson of Tritone Guitars and I had been friends for many years when one day we both asked the question, "What would be the perfect club and studio amp that could cover most all bases for blues, country, roots rock/Americana?"

We both eventually agreed that pound-for-pound, the blackface Princeton Reverb™ would be hard to beat, but it simply lacked the power and girth to make it in a club with a loud drummer. I agreed to set about re-designing the Princeton Reverb™ to not only boost its power and girth, but improve it's tone overall while also making it more versatile.

The Longbeard Tritone was thus born.

Tritone Stats

The Longbeard Tritone is a handwired 20w, 12" combo amp made from the most top-quality components — including Mallory™ and Sprague™ capacitors, carbon film resistors, Carling™ switches, and Switchcraft™ jacks — and housed in a handbuilt pine cabinet, with wheat grill, covered in your choice of black, blonde, brown, red, green, or blue covering. Running on dual 6V6 power tubes, 12AX7 (3x) and 12AT7 preamp tubes, and a 5U4 rectifier, the Tritone features adjustable fixed bias, spring Reverb with dwell control, bias modulated tremolo, and a "Soul" control to switch from "Tight (blackface)" to "Loose (tweed)" tones. External footswitch jacks (for vibrato and reverb), bass and treble controls, and high and low impedance inputs round out the Tritone's features to make it an incredibly portable, versatile, and gig-ready amplifier.

Contact Longbeard

My amp building passion came about out of necessity while working as an office products repairman in my twenties. Since I couldn't afford the vintage amps I truly wanted, I utilized my electronics training to set about modifying affordable silverface Fender amps to achieve the vibe of the over-inflated vintage showpieces that I could only borrow but never purchase. Over time, modification turned into engineering and building from the ground up.

- Scott Kuhnert, Owner/Operator
